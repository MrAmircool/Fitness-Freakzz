Vinyasa yoga Description:

Vinyasa yoga offers a dynamic and transformative practice that nourishes the body, mind, and spirit through movement, breath, and mindfulness:

  • Flowing Movement: Practitioners move through a series of poses in a connected sequence, promoting flexibility, strength, and balance.
  • Breath Awareness: Being aware of the breath's movement in and out of the body, noticing its pace, depth, and quality.
  • Sequencing:Often incorporated to further warm up the body and create a rhythmic flow of movement and breath.

Hatha yoga

Watch on YouTube

Hatha yoga Description:

Hatha yoga is a traditional form of yoga that focuses on physical postures, breath control, and meditation:

  • Physical Postures (Asanas): Hatha yoga involves a series of physical postures or asanas that help to strengthen and stretch the body.
    These postures are designed to improve flexibility, balance, and overall physical well-being.
  • Breath Control (Pranayama): Pranayama, or breath control, is an essential component of Hatha yoga.
    Practitioners learn various breathing techniques to regulate and deepen their breath, promoting relaxation and focus.
  • Mind-Body Connection: Hatha yoga emphasizes the connection between the mind and body.
    Practicing asanas and breath control techniques helps to calm the mind, reduce stress, and enhance concentration.

Iyengar yoga Description:

yengar yoga emphasizes correct alignment of the body in each yoga pose. The practitioner pays attention to the alignment of bones, muscles, and joints to achieve the proper posture.

  • Alignment: yengar yoga emphasizes correct alignment of the body in each yoga pose. The practitioner pays attention to the alignment of bones, muscles, and joints to achieve the proper posture.
  • Sequencing::Poses are often sequenced in a specific order to build strength, flexibility, and balance progressively. The sequence may vary depending on the level of the practitioner and the focus of the practice.
  • Breath Awareness:Iyengar yoga emphasizes the importance of breath awareness (pranayama) during practice. Practitioners learn to synchronize their breath with movement and to deepen their breath to enhance relaxation and concentration.

Kundalini yoga Description:

Kundalini Yoga is a spiritual and physical practice that aims to awaken the dormant energy located
at the base of the spine (known as Kundalini energy) through various techniques including postures, breathwork, meditation, and chanting.

  • Exercise 1: Kundalini Energy ... Central to Kundalini Yoga is the belief in the existence of a dormant spiritual energy, known as Kundalini,
    which resides at the base of the spine, often depicted as a coiled serpent.
  • Exercise 2: Holistic Approach. Kundalini Yoga is a holistic practice that integrates physical, mental, and spiritual elements,
    aiming to achieve balance, harmony, and alignment of the body, mind, and spirit.

Ashtanga yoga Description:

Ashtanga yoga is a traditional form of yoga that follows a set sequence of postures and breathing techniques
designed to create internal heat and detoxify the body

  • Exercise 1:Vinyasa... Ashtanga yoga utilizes a specific vinyasa system, which is the synchronization of breath with movement.
    Each movement is linked with either an inhale or an exhale.
  • Exercise 2:Teacher-Led Practice... Traditionally, Ashtanga yoga is taught in a teacher-led class format.
    where students practice the sequence together in a group setting, led by a certified instructor.
